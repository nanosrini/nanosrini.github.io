About

I completed Master's in Microbiology from Kristu Jayanti College, Bengaluru. My major research interest is on nanotechnology, cancer biology and bioinformatics. After college, i joined as Project Assistant (JRF) at CSIR-CECRI, where i was analysing Biofilms in drinking water. Later i joined ICMR-NIV during the COVID-19 pandemic and was into diagnosis of various vial diseases. After that, i joined as SRF in NIMHANS, where i worked on identification of Biomarker for meningitis. I like to integrate microbiology and nanotechnology with bioinformatics, for rapid diagnosis. I like to work in the field of nanotechnology for biosensors, bio-imagining and create rapid diagnostic kits for various infections..

Research

My first work during my master’s thesis was on nanoparticles mediated gene delivery, where I synthesised super paramagnetic iron oxide nanoparticles and utilised for gene delivery applications. Later during my JRF, I was analysing how biofilms form on different pipe materials in drinking water and how do they affect the water quality. I was also utilising Foldscope: a paper microscope for visualising the microbes in water. During my work in National Institute of Virology during the COVID-19 pandemic apart from routine diagnostic work, I was trying to do a extraction free PCR for SARS-CoV-2 virus and perform a rapid extraction of nucleic acid without centrifugation. I was not successful in performing a extraction free PCR, but the results for the rapid nucleic acid extraction were good, unfortunately I was not able to get funds to go ahead with this research. .
